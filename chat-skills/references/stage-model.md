# Stage Model

## 1. Stranger Contact

Goal:

- get a reply without feeling generic or invasive

Green flags:

- she answers the actual question
- she adds one detail
- she responds within a normal rhythm

Avoid:

- long self-intros
- sexual framing
- multi-question walls

Recommended move:

- observation + light question

## 2. Warm-Up

Goal:

- establish conversational rhythm
- prove you can carry a normal exchange

Green flags:

- she asks back
- she teases or reacts emotionally
- she uses fuller sentences

Avoid:

- interrogating
- over-performing
- forcing flirtation too early

Recommended move:

- thread expansion + shared context

## 3. Comfort Building

Goal:

- create familiarity and low-pressure trust

Green flags:

- she volunteers personal details
- she references prior messages
- she responds to your emotional observations

Avoid:

- oversharing too fast
- heavy trauma dumping
- turning every topic into relationship talk

Recommended move:

- specific noticing + grounded empathy

## 4. Playful Tension

Goal:

- make the interaction feel distinct from ordinary friendship

Green flags:

- she playfully pushes back
- she accepts mild teasing
- she allows ambiguity and flirtation

Avoid:

- negging
- trying to "win"
- escalating after flat responses

Recommended move:

- playful contrast + light challenge + easy exit

## 5. Invite Window

Goal:

- move from abstract interaction to a concrete plan

Green flags:

- she replies consistently
- she makes time references
- she engages with location or activity ideas

Avoid:

- vague "sometime we should"
- giant planning essays
- demanding justification for schedule conflicts

Recommended move:

- simple activity + clear time frame + low-pressure framing

## 6. Post-Date Momentum

Goal:

- reinforce the positive experience without over-chasing

Green flags:

- she references a moment from the date
- she uses future-oriented language
- she replies with warmth rather than pure politeness

Avoid:

- post-date overanalysis
- immediate exclusivity talk
- sending essays because you are excited

Recommended move:

- callback + short appreciation + light future hook

## 7. Conflict or Fadeout

Goal:

- reduce damage, clarify reality, and preserve dignity

Green flags:

- she still replies with substance
- she names a real concern
- she leaves a door open

Avoid:

- defensive paragraphs
- moral accusations
- panic escalation

Recommended move:

- acknowledge + clarify + one bounded next step

## Transition rule

Advance a stage when behavior supports it, not because you are impatient.

Use this order:

- comfort before tension
- tension before logistics
- logistics before intimacy
- explicit enthusiasm before sexual escalation
